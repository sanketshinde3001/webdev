Discover the Natural Heritage of Sahyadri Hills !

Sahyadri Hills, also known as the Western Ghats, is a mountain range located along the western coast of India. It stretches for approximately 1,600 kilometers (990 miles) from the state of Gujarat in the north to the southern tip of Tamil Nadu. Sahyadri Hills is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site due to its immense biological diversity and ecological importance.

Sahyadri hills

In addition to its natural beauty, Sahyadri Hills holds a significant cultural and historical legacy. It is dotted with ancient temples, caves, and fortresses that bear witness to the region's rich heritage. Many of these historical sites are nestled amidst the hills, offering a unique blend of nature and history for visitors to explore.

MATHERAN

Matheran is a popular hill station and one of the smallest hill stations in India. Known for its stunning panoramic views, Matheran offers a unique experience as vehicles are not allowed within the town, preserving its natural beauty.

MAHABALE- SHWAR

Mahabaleshwar is a renowned hill station and a favored getaway in Sahyadri Hills. It is famous for its strawberry farms, breathtaking viewpoints like Arthur's Seat and Kate's Point, and the stunning Venna Lake.

Fort Rajmachi

Located in the Sahyadri range, Fort Rajmachi is a historic site that attracts adventure enthusiasts and history lovers. The fort offers magnificent views of the surrounding valleys and is accessible through a trek.
